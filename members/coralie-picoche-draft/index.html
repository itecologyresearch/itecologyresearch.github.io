Coralie Picoche

I am an ecologist with a background in mathematics and informatics applied to biological systems. I used to work on species-specific as well as end-to-end models in applied contexts, mostly in aquatic environments for which I have developed a specific interest.

For the last years, my research has focused on explaining the coexistence of multiple species competing for similar resources. I have sought confirmation of already known theoretical mechanisms from field data, and attempted to further theory by analysing the effect of observed processes in more theoretical frameworks. To do so, I have used both statistical tools and mechanistic models.

Currently, as a PhD student, I specialize on phytoplankton community. Popular phytoplankton models tend to use global variables (such as biomasses of large groups of vegetative organisms), with large spatial and temporal scales. On the contrary, I aim to include life histories in phytoplankton models, and to represent fine-scale hydrodynamic effects, with a specific focus on the way turbulence can support coexistence by spatially segregating organisms.
